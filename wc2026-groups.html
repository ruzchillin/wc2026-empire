🆕 The New Format — Everything Changed in 2026

48 Teams · 3 Per Group · More Upsets Than Ever

WC 2026 is the first 48-team World Cup. 12 groups of 4, with the top 3 from each group advancing (plus 8 best third-placed teams at some formats — check official FIFA rules). This creates a totally different betting landscape: lower-ranked teams have more chance to survive the group stage, group winner markets are tighter, and the "death group" concept intensifies.

Key betting implications: Group winner markets are more valuable because strong seeds face weaker opposition in 4-team groups. A guaranteed minnow in each group means Brazil, France, Spain, England are near-certainties to top their groups — but at short odds. The value is in second-place qualifier markets and group winner exact margins.
