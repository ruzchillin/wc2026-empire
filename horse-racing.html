Form Guide Jargon Buster
Every term a professional uses — explained in plain English
Going
Track surface condition. Firm = fast, hard ground (suits front runners). Soft = slow, wet (suits stamina horses). Good to Firm is the most common.
Form figures
Numbers showing finish positions, most recent first. "1-2-1-3" = won, 2nd, won, 3rd. "0" = unplaced. "F" = fell. "P" = pulled up.
Trainer strike rate
% of runners that win at a given track. A trainer with 25% strike rate at Ascot wins 1 in 4 runners there — far above average (10%).
Top weight
Horse carrying the most weight in a handicap. Gives the others a chance but also signals it's rated highest. Top weight horses win more often than their price suggests.
Ante-post
Betting months before a race. Better prices but no non-runner money back. The AI identifies when ante-post prices are mispriced after major trials.
Lay bet
Betfair Exchange only. You bet AGAINST a horse winning. If it doesn't win, you collect the stake. You're acting as the bookmaker for that bet.
Each way
Two bets in one — half on win, half on place (top 3 usually, more in big fields). If the horse places but doesn't win, you get 1/4 or 1/5 the win odds back on the place part.
In-running / in-play
Betting while the race is happening. Betfair Exchange prices move wildly during the race — experienced traders back horses that stumble early but are still running well.
Best Odds Guaranteed (BOG)
If you take an early price and the SP (starting price at the off) is bigger, the bookmaker pays the bigger price. Bet365, William Hill, Coral all offer BOG on UK/Irish racing.
Sectional times
Split times through each portion of the race. A horse that runs a fast final 2 furlongs (a "strong finisher") is likely to improve stepping up in trip.
Handicap mark
Official Rating (OR) — a number from 0–140 assigning weight to horses. The higher the OR, the more weight they carry. An OR of 100 is a decent handicapper; 120+ is Group class.
RPR / Timeform / BHA rating
Three parallel rating systems (Racing Post Rating, Timeform, British Horseracing Authority official). All measure performance on a comparable scale. Our AI uses all three.
