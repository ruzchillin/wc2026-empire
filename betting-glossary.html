Betting Glossary

Every football betting term explained in plain English. From accumulator to Asian handicap — master the language before WC 2026 kicks off.

ABCDEFGHILMOPRSTVW

A

Accumulator (Acca)

A single bet that combines multiple selections. All selections must win for the bet to pay out. The odds multiply together, creating large potential returns from a small stake.

Example: Brazil to win + France to win + England to win = 3-fold acca. If all three win, you get 2/1 × 2/1 × 5/2 = 27.5/1 on your stake.

Asian Handicap

A betting type where the favourite gives a goal head-start to the underdog, eliminating the draw outcome. Bets can be on half-goal or quarter-goal handicaps, meaning no push (draw) is possible.

Example: Brazil -1.5 Asian Handicap means Brazil must win by 2+ goals for the bet to win. Brazil win 2-0 = WIN. Brazil win 1-0 = LOSE.

Ante-Post

Bets placed before a tournament starts, often at higher odds than day-of betting. WC 2026 ante-post prices are available now — you get the best odds but face the risk of team/player changes.

Example: Back Mbappé for Golden Boot at 9/2 today (ante-post). By tournament day, if he's form, this might shorten to 3/1.

B

BTTS (Both Teams to Score)

A market where you bet on whether both teams will score at least one goal in a match. "BTTS Yes" pays if both teams score; "BTTS No" pays if either team keeps a clean sheet.

Example: France vs Germany. BTTS Yes at 13/10. France score (1-0), Germany score (1-1). BTTS Yes = WIN.

Banker

A selection you're highly confident about — the cornerstone of an accumulator. A "banker bet" is one you'd stake more on, or the leg of an acca you're most certain about.

Example: Brazil vs minnow team — "Brazil to win" is your banker. You build the acca around it.

Bankroll

Your total betting budget. Professional bettors never stake more than 1-5% of bankroll on a single bet, regardless of how confident they are. Protects against variance.

Example: £200 bankroll → maximum stake per bet = £10 (5%). Never chase losses with the full bankroll.

C

Cash Out

A feature offered by most bookmakers allowing you to settle a bet before the event ends, at a value calculated from the current odds. Use it to lock in profits when ahead, or cut losses when behind.

Example: You back Brazil at 6/1 before the tournament. They reach the semi-final. Cash out value: £85 on a £10 stake, rather than waiting for the final (which could be £70 or £110).

Correct Score

A bet predicting the exact final score of a match. Higher-odds market due to difficulty. "Brazil 2-1" might be 12/1 — potentially good value if you can assess true probability.

Example: Brazil vs lower seed: 2-0 at 4/1, 3-0 at 7/1, 2-1 at 8/1. AI models can identify the most likely correct score.

Coupon

Your bet slip — the list of selections you've added before placing a bet. Term used primarily in UK/Irish betting culture. "Building a coupon" means adding selections to your bet slip.

Example: "I've got a 5-team coupon for Saturday's group stage games."

E

Each Way (E/W)

A two-part bet: one part on the selection to win, one part on it to place (e.g. finish top-2, top-3). In football tournament betting, "each way" means win OR reach the semi-final. See our full Each-Way Guide →

Example: Morocco each-way at 80/1 (1/5 odds a place = 16/1 for SF). If Morocco reach SF = win at 16/1 on the place part.

Enhanced Odds

A promotional price offered by a bookmaker, better than their standard odds. Common WC promotions: "England to win first match boosted to 2/1" (from 4/7). Great for first-time sign-ups.

Example: Sky Bet offer: Brazil first goal in 30 min normally 6/4 — enhanced to 9/4 for new customers.

F

Form

A team or player's recent results and performance. "In form" means performing well recently. Bookmakers price odds partly on form — but their models may lag recent data, creating value opportunities.

Example: Colombia won 10 of last 12 games. Their 40/1 WC odds may undervalue current form.

Fractional Odds

The UK standard for expressing odds. "5/1" means for every £1 you stake, you win £5 profit (plus your £1 stake back). Also written as "five-to-one."

Example: Messi at 12/1 to win Golden Boot: stake £10, win £120 + £10 stake = £130 total return.

Free Bet

A promotional bet funded by the bookmaker, not from your balance. Free bet winnings (profit only) are yours to keep. See our Free Bets 2026 page → for all current offers.

Example: Deposit £10, get a £10 free bet. Use the free bet on Morocco 80/1. Win = £800 free bet winnings (stake not returned on most offers).

H

Handicap

A market that gives the favourite a deficit to overcome, or the underdog a head-start. Levels the playing field in one-sided matches. European handicap includes draw; Asian handicap eliminates draw.

Example: Brazil -1 handicap vs weaker team: Brazil must win by 2+ goals. Brazil win 1-0 = lose. Brazil win 2-0 = win.

Half-Time/Full-Time (HT/FT)

A market predicting both the half-time and full-time result. Much higher odds than straight match result. "France/France" (lead at HT and win at FT) might be 4/5 vs 4/6 for France to just win.

Example: England leading at HT and winning FT = "England/England" at 11/8. In SGMs this multiplies with other selections.

I

In-Play (Live Betting)

Betting on a match after it has started, with odds updating in real time. See our full In-Play Betting Guide → for strategy and best markets.

Example: Spain 0-0 at 30 min → bet Spain to win in-play at 4/5 (was 1/2 pre-match, now better value).

Implied Probability

The probability a bookmaker's odds imply. 5/1 odds = 1/6 = 16.7% implied probability. If your research suggests the true probability is higher, the bet has value.

Example: Morocco at 80/1 = 1.2% implied probability. Our AI gives Morocco a 3% win probability = value bet.

O

Odds

The price a bookmaker offers for an outcome. Fractional (5/1), Decimal (6.0), or American (+500). All express the same thing: the return on a winning bet relative to stake.

Example: 5/1 = 6.00 (decimal) = +500 (American). All mean: win £5 profit for every £1 staked.

Outright

A bet on the overall tournament winner, top scorer, best young player etc. — as opposed to a single match result. WC 2026 outrights include: Winner, Golden Boot, Best Young Player, Golden Glove.

Example: Brazil to win WC 2026 at 6/1 is an outright bet. See Outright Winner Guide →

Overround

The bookmaker's built-in margin on a market. If all probabilities in a market add up to more than 100%, the excess is the bookmaker's margin. The lower the overround, the better value for the bettor.

Example: 3-way match result: Home 40% + Draw 30% + Away 30% = 100% (no margin). Bookmaker versions: 43% + 32% + 32% = 107% — 7% overround (margin).

S

Same Game Multi (SGM)

An accumulator where all selections come from the same match. Available at bet365, Sky Bet, William Hill. Higher risk than regular accas but great returns on correlated outcomes in one game.

Example: Brazil win + Vinícius anytime scorer + Over 2.5 goals + BTTS = SGM at 12/1 for a single Brazil match.

Single

A bet on one selection only. The simplest bet type. Lower variance than accas. Long-term, successful bettors primarily use singles over accas for better bankroll management.

Example: £10 single on France to win their group at 4/7. Returns £15.71 if they do.

Spread Betting

Betting on a continuous outcome (e.g. total goals in a tournament) rather than a yes/no event. Profit or loss depends on how far from the spread the result is. Higher risk, unlimited upside/downside.

Example: Total goals in WC 2026 spread: 170-175. Buy at 175 at £5/goal. If 195 goals = £100 profit. If 155 goals = £100 loss.

V

Value Bet

A bet where the true probability of an outcome is higher than the implied probability in the odds. The foundation of long-term profitable betting. Our AI model identifies value across all WC 2026 markets.

Example: Morocco at 80/1 = 1.2% implied probability. AI gives Morocco 3% win probability = value of 2.5× — strong value bet.

Vigorish (Vig / Juice)

The bookmaker's commission built into odds. Equivalent to the overround. In exchange betting, the vig is the commission charged on winning bets (e.g. Betfair charges 5% commission on winnings).

Example: Betfair exchange: you win £100 on a bet. Betfair takes 5% commission = £5. Net profit = £95.
