🏆 The Legacy — Why This Final WC Matters

From War-Torn Childhood to the World Stage

Modrić's story begins in Zadar, Croatia, during the Yugoslav Wars. His grandfather was killed. His family were refugees. Yet from those beginnings emerged a player who would win 6 Champions Leagues, be named the world's best player (2018 Ballon d'Or), and carry a nation of 4 million to a World Cup final.

In 2018, every pundit dismissed Croatia before the tournament. Modrić proved them all wrong. He orchestrated wins over Argentina, Russia, England, and lost only in the final to France. In 2022, at 36, he did it again — reaching 3rd place while most of his Madrid teammates had retired from international football.

At WC 2026, Modrić is 40. No player this old has ever captained a team to a WC semi-final.
