🤖 Why Trap Bias Matters — The Core Edge
Every greyhound track has bends. At a bend, the dog on the inside rail runs less distance. At shorter distances (380m has one full bend), the trap draw from Trap 1 is the most statistically significant variable — more predictive than recent form, weight, or trainer in many cases. This is why professional greyhound traders on Betfair focus so heavily on trap statistics. The AI tracks every UK and Irish track's trap bias at every distance, updated daily. When a high-quality dog gets a favourable trap draw, that convergence is the highest-confidence situation in greyhound betting.

Trap Bias Backing
Find a dog in a high-bias trap (T1 Romford, T3 Sheffield) with recent form that suits. Back on Exchange pre-race at 3.0–5.0. With trap edge + form, expected value is positive even at these shorter prices.
Lay the Overpriced Favourite
Books set greyhound favourite at ~2.0 for the market leader. On Exchange, that same dog is often 2.4–2.8. Laying that dog when its trap is unfavourable creates a consistent edge. Works best at Sheffield T3 vs T5/T6 favourites.
Grade Drop Betting
When a dog drops from A2 → A3 or A3 → A4, it's almost always because of a trainer decision to target a win. These "hot drops" win at 35–40% strike rate. Back at 3.0 or better. Scale up stakes on high-confidence drops.
In-Running Trading
Back a dog at 2.0 pre-race. If it breaks well and leads after 5 seconds, the price on Exchange drops to 1.3–1.5. Trade out for a guaranteed profit regardless of result. Even a 50% success rate at 2.0→1.4 is highly profitable.

Greyhound Racing Jargon — Plain English
Every term you need to understand form and tips
TermPlain English Meaning
BAGSBritish Association of Greyhound Studios — afternoon meetings broadcast to betting shops. Run all day. Most volume on Betfair.
Trap drawWhich of the 6 starting boxes a dog runs from. Numbered T1 (inside rail) to T6 (outside). Fixed before the race. The AI's main input.
Grade (A1–A8)A1 is elite Open class; A8 is beginner. Grade A drop (e.g., A3→A4) is the single most reliable "hot horse" signal. A rise means harder competition.
GoingTrack surface condition — Fast, Standard, Slow, Heavy. Similar to horse racing going. Fast = dry, hard track. Heavy = wet, slower times.
Wide runnerA dog that naturally takes a wide path through the bends. Disadvantaged from T1 (rail), best from T4-T6. The opposite is a railer.
RailerA dog that hugs the inside rail through bends. Naturally suited to T1 or T2. From an outside trap, a railer will cut across rivals — danger of interference.
PB (Personal Best)Fastest time ever run by that dog at that distance/track. The single most objective measure of ability.
Sectional timeSplit time at a point in the race (typically the bend). A dog with fast sectionals from the middle is accelerating through the race — a strong sign.
Open raceThe highest class of greyhound race — no grade restrictions. Invitational. Similar to a Group 1 in horse racing. Winners worth large amounts in prize money.
KnuckleWhen a dog's leg clips the mechanical lure or a rail, causing it to stumble or slow. Unpreventable but important in form reading — discount a performance where a knuckle was noted.
