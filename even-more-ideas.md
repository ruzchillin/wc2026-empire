## CATEGORY 8: PSYCHOLOGICAL & SOCIAL MECHANICS

**36. Group Prediction Leagues (Private Mini-Leagues)**
Like FPL mini-leagues: users create a private league, invite friends, compete head-to-head.

This is how prediction games go exponentially viral. Each user invites 5 friends → each friend invites 5 more → geometric growth.
Add: workplace leagues, family leagues, school leagues as named templates.
Revenue: free to create, premium features (custom prizes, extended history, live leaderboard on TV display).

**37. The Shame Board**
A public leaderboard of the most confidently wrong predictions of the tournament.

Why it works: self-deprecating humor is the most shared content on social media. People voluntarily submit their worst calls. It goes viral because football fans are funny about their own suffering.
Revenue: email capture to submit. Traffic spike every time a big upset happens.

**38. Prediction Replay (Spotify Wrapped for WC)**
On July 20 (day after the Final), send every user their personal WC prediction recap:
- Total predictions made
- Accuracy score
- Best call (your most confident correct prediction)
- Worst call (most confident wrong prediction)
- How you compare vs 100,000 other users globally

Shareable card (like Spotify Wrapped). Drives massive social sharing in July–August. Keeps your brand alive and growing after the tournament ends.

**39. "Pick the AI" Game**
Reverse the Beat the AI: show users three possible predictions for tomorrow's match and ask them to guess which one is the actual AI output.

This gamifies the prediction process, teaches users how the AI thinks, and goes viral because it makes you feel clever when you're right.
Email capture to play. No technical complexity.

**40. The Legend Badge System**
Verified accuracy tiers displayed on every user's profile:
- 55%+ accuracy: "Analyst"
- 60%+: "Expert"
- 65%+: "Sharp"
- 70%+: "Oracle"
- 75%+: "Legend" (very few achieve this)

Badges are public. People grind throughout the tournament to achieve them.
Revenue: the badge system drives daily return visits, which drives affiliate click-throughs.

**41. Confidence Calibration — The Proof Nobody Else Has**
Most tipsters just give picks. You give confidence scores (68%, 71%, etc.).

Now track publicly: when you say 70% confidence, do you actually win 70% of the time?

This is called "calibration" and it's what separates honest prediction from nonsense.
Publish your calibration chart weekly during WC. If you're well-calibrated: this is the most powerful marketing you have.
It's scientifically verifiable. It's public. It's genuinely hard to fake.
When journalists write about prediction markets during WC, your calibration data is the story.

**42. Player Contract / Salary Content**
Fans are obsessed with player salaries. "How much does [player] earn?" is searched millions of times.
AdSense on salary content runs at high RPM because it's aspirational.
Content: "WC 2026 player salary rankings," "Who is the highest-paid player at WC 2026."
Traffic: perpetual. Salary content stays relevant for years.

**43. Manager Profile Series**
WC managers are storylines. When a manager gets fired mid-tournament (almost guaranteed), your pre-built deep profile is the only detailed source = massive traffic spike.

Build before June 11: profiles of all 48 WC 2026 managers.
Revenue: AdSense traffic spike every time one gets fired or does something controversial.
