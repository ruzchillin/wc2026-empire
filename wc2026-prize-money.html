❓ FAQ

Do players get the prize money directly? +
No — prize money is paid to the national football association (FA), not to individual players. Each FA then decides how much to share with its players and coaching staff. Some countries (England, France) pay bonuses of £50,000-£100,000+ per player. Others retain more for infrastructure.
How does WC2026 prize money compare to club football? +
The $50m winner's prize dwarfs club prizes. The 2025/26 Champions League winner earns around €130m in UEFA distributions, but that's spread over a whole season of matches. The WC2026 winner earns $50m for ~7 games over 6 weeks.
Why is the 2026 prize fund so much bigger? +
Three reasons: (1) the tournament has 48 teams instead of 32, meaning more games and more broadcast revenue; (2) the USA market brings in massive additional sponsorship and TV rights; (3) FIFA's commercial revenue has grown significantly since 2022 — the total fund is more than double Qatar 2022's $440m.
When is prize money paid? +
FIFA pays prize money to football associations in multiple tranches — a participation fee paid before the tournament, and round-by-round payments as teams progress. The final payment is made after the tournament concludes.
