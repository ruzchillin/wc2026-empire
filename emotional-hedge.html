Connect a hedging account (Betfair Exchange recommended)
The hedge needs a funded exchange account to work automatically. Betfair Exchange is ideal — you're not betting on your team to lose with a sportsbook, you're placing a lay bet on the exchange at better prices. We never touch your balance except for automated hedge placement.

Set your hedge parameters
Choose your hedge level: Full cover (maximum payout if they lose, zero if they win), Partial cover (soften the blow either way), or Break-even (protect your matchday spend regardless of result). Our AI calculates the optimal hedge amount for each match automatically.

The system monitors odds and places the hedge
Before every match your team plays, the system checks the live odds on Betfair Exchange. If your team is favourite or at a price where the hedge makes mathematical sense, it places automatically at the configured amount. You get a confirmation notification — nothing required from you.

Your team wins: the hedge loses its stake but your emotional state is great. Your team loses or draws: the hedge pays out. Betfair settles automatically — the funds appear in your exchange account within minutes of the final whistle.

The science behind it
Why the hedge works on your brain, not just your wallet.
🧠
Loss aversion is 2× stronger than gain
Behavioural economics (Kahneman & Tversky) shows that losses feel twice as bad as equivalent gains feel good. Hedging doesn't eliminate the loss — it adds a gain that partially cancels the emotional sting.

💡
Knowing you'll be compensated changes the experience
Studies on anticipatory emotion show that knowing a bad outcome triggers compensation measurably reduces its emotional impact — before the result is even in. You watch the game differently when you know the hedge exists.

This is the question almost everyone asks first. The honest answer: you're not betting on your team to lose — you're protecting your financial position, the same way a homeowner insures against fire while hoping the house never burns. Your emotional support for the team is completely unchanged. The hedge is a financial instrument, not an allegiance statement. Thousands of season ticket holders do this every week.
If your team wins, you lose the hedge stake — but you get the result you actually wanted. Think of it like car insurance: when you don't crash, you don't get the insurance money back. You paid for peace of mind, and the best outcome happened. The hedge only costs you the stake when the good result occurs. For a £15 stake, you pay £15 for the peace of mind of knowing a loss won't ruin your weekend. Many fans find that's worth it regardless of result.
The underlying mechanics are similar to matched betting (laying a team on the exchange at better odds than backing them at a sportsbook). The difference is: this product does all the calculation, all the placement, and all the monitoring for you. You never have to understand how it works at a technical level. You just set up which team, how much to protect, and the system handles everything. It's matched betting made accessible to fans who don't want to learn the maths.
The hedge stake depends on three things: your team's odds (favourites have smaller payouts so you need a larger stake), your protection level, and your target payout.
