🆓 Can I Watch for Free?

📺

Yes — Fox Sports broadcasts many matches free over the air

If you have a TV antenna, all Fox-broadcast matches (including the Final) air free on local Fox affiliates. Fox is available over-the-air in every US market. Spanish matches on Telemundo are also free over the air.

No antenna? Tubi (free, no subscription) will carry select matches. The Fox Sports app streams free with a cable login — or you can use a streaming service free trial (see below).

Match windows: Group stage games kick off at 12pm, 3pm, 6pm, and 9pm ET — all at times Americans can actually watch. This is the most watch-friendly World Cup ever hosted in the US.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How many matches are in WC 2026?
104 total matches — the largest World Cup ever. Group stage runs June 11 – July 2, knockout rounds July 3 – July 19. The Final is July 19 at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey.
Which channel has the most WC 2026 matches?
Fox and FS1 carry all English-language coverage. Telemundo and Peacock carry all Spanish-language coverage. FuboTV carries both, making it the single best option for WC streaming.
Can I watch the WC 2026 Final for free?
Yes — the Final airs on Fox, which is free over-the-air with a TV antenna. If you don't have an antenna, start a free trial on any service listed above and cancel before you're charged.
What's the cheapest way to watch all 104 matches?
Combine: TV antenna for Fox/Telemundo matches (free) + Peacock at $10.99/mo for streaming Telemundo games. That covers most matches. For full coverage, FuboTV at $45.99/mo is the best value.
What time do WC 2026 matches start?
Group stage kick-off times: 12:00 PM ET, 3:00 PM ET, 6:00 PM ET, and 9:00 PM ET. The US-friendly schedule is one of the biggest advantages of this North American tournament.
